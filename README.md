# Budongo-study
# Long-term Impact of Selective Logging on Tropical Tree Species Diversity

**MSc Thesis | Georg-August University Göttingen | October 2024**
**Author:** Joseph Ghartey
**Supervisors:** Dr. Martin Ehbrecht & Dr. Najeeb Al-Amin Iddris

## Study Site
Budongo Forest Reserve, Uganda — comparing logged (N3) 
and unlogged (N15) compartments after 70 years of recovery.

## Repository Contents
- Raw soil and species data
- Fully annotated R scripts for all analyses
- Output figures and tables
- Full thesis PDF

## Key Findings
- Logged forest showed higher species richness & evenness
- Structural recovery achieved after 70 years
- Canopy composition still carries disturbance signature
- Aluminium emerged as dominant soil driver in logged stands
